Instrument 02 — Decision Trace
Exercise 2 · 20 minutes  |  One card per person. Capture the why behind a real decision.
The Prompt
Think of a real decision from your current project —
ideally one that caused confusion, rework, or a question you couldn't fully answer later.
It does not have to be a big decision. Sprint scope changes, design direction calls, tool choices, and priority shifts all count. The smaller and more everyday the decision, the more useful this exercise is.
Instrument 02 · Decision Trace
The Five Fields
8 min individual
01 · WHAT — Exactly what was agreed. Active voice, past tense.
02 · WHY — The specific reasoning. Not backstory — the logic that justified this over alternatives.
03 · TRADEOFF — What was gained. What was sacrificed. Every real decision has one.
04 · OUTCOME — Which project goal does this decision serve?
05 · CONFIDENCE — How explicitly was this agreed?
1
Implicit — assumed, never stated aloud

Informal — verbal agreement, no record

Explicit — agreed with full reasoning, documented

Facilitator instructions:
1Individual: complete all five fields for a real decision. (8 min)
2Pair up. Each person reads their tradeoff aloud. Partner asks: "Does that feel honest — or is there something you're softening?" (5 min each)
3Room: which field was hardest? (Answer is almost always WHY or TRADEOFF.) (5 min)
4Ask: if this trace had existed before the work began — would anything have been different?

Five takeaways  ·  Reframe 2026
